# EcoSync Pitch Plan

## The Core Story Arc

> "Every year, North America sends **600 million tons** of construction waste to landfills. Up to 30% of that is perfectly usable material — thrown away because there's no easy way to move it to the next job site."

We're not pitching a marketplace. We're pitching the **missing infrastructure** for a circular construction economy.

---

## (A) Prove the Market Exists

### Construction & Demolition (C&D) Waste is a Monster Market

- C&D waste makes up **25–40% of all solid waste** in North America (EPA)
- The US alone generates **600M+ tons of C&D debris annually** — more than double municipal solid waste (EPA 2018)
- Canada generates ~4M tonnes of C&D waste/year, with only ~30% diverted from landfills
- The global C&D waste management market is valued at **$200B+** and growing at ~5% CAGR

### Narrow Focus: Lumber & Steel Surplus

Per the mentor feedback — don't try to cover everything. Pick 1–2 materials and go deep.

**Why lumber:**
- Lumber accounts for ~20–30% of C&D waste by volume
- Contractors routinely over-order by 10–15% as a buffer — that surplus has nowhere to go
- Reusable lumber sells at 30–50% discount vs. new — there's real buyer demand
- Lumber prices have been volatile (300% spike during COVID) — contractors want cheaper sourcing options

**Why steel:**
- Steel is the most recycled material on earth (~90% recycling rate globally), proving there IS a market for reuse
- But small/mid contractors lack access to efficient resale channels — large scrapyards dominate
- Structural steel and rebar are high-value, standardized, and easy to list/verify

### The Gap We Fill

Material resale exists, but it's fragmented: Craigslist postings, word-of-mouth, scrapyard lowball offers. There is **no dedicated, geo-local, verified platform** for contractors to trade surplus. That's the gap.

## (B) Show It's a Big Problem

### The Waste Problem (Environmental)

- Construction waste occupies **~30% of all landfill space** in the US
- C&D waste contributes significantly to embodied carbon — the carbon already spent manufacturing materials that then get thrown away
- A single demolished house generates ~50–80 tons of waste; most of it could be reused

### The Cost Problem (Economic)

- Contractors lose **$2–4B/year** on surplus materials they can't offload efficiently
- Disposal fees (tipping fees) range from **$40–$100/ton** — contractors PAY to throw away usable material
- Small-to-mid builders are squeezed: materials cost more, and they can't absorb waste losses the way large GCs can

### The Compliance Problem (Regulatory)

- "Buy Clean" laws are spreading: California (SB 253), federal infrastructure bill requirements, GSA mandates
- Builders must now report **embodied carbon** in materials — but have no tools to do this easily
- Cities like Vancouver, NYC, and Portland are adding C&D diversion mandates (some requiring **75%+ diversion from landfill**)
- Non-compliance = permit delays, fines, lost contracts

### Frame it as a Triple Crisis

> "Contractors are paying to throw away usable materials, while the builder down the street is paying full price for the exact same thing. Meanwhile, regulators are tightening the rules and nobody has the tools to comply. This is a market failure — and it's costing billions."

## (C) Case Studies & Proof Points

### 1. Rheaply (Chicago) — B2B Asset Reuse Platform
- Raised $18.75M+ in venture funding
- Helps organizations redistribute surplus assets internally and externally
- **Proves:** the B2B reuse marketplace model is venture-backable and scalable
- **Our differentiator:** Rheaply is broad (office furniture, lab equipment); EcoSync is laser-focused on construction

### 2. Planet Reuse / Roofstock of Materials
- Platforms that have attempted construction material resale with varying success
- The ones that failed were too broad or too manual
- **Lesson for us:** Geo-local focus + AI-powered listing (photo → material type → price) reduces friction to near zero

### 3. The EC3 (Embodied Carbon in Construction Calculator)
- Free, open-source tool backed by the Building Transparency Foundation
- Contains EPD (Environmental Product Declaration) data for 100K+ construction products
- **We integrate with this** — every transaction on EcoSync auto-calculates carbon saved
- This is a real, existing API that makes our carbon tracking credible, not hand-wavy

### 4. Buy Clean California Act
- First state-level policy requiring embodied carbon reporting for certain building materials (steel, glass, insulation)
- Since passed, **multiple states + federal govt** have followed with similar mandates
- **Proves:** the regulatory tailwind is real and growing — builders NEED tools for this

### 5. Past HTG Winners Show This Pattern Works
- 2025 2nd Place: second-hand clothing marketplace (same model — surplus goods platform)
- 2024 1st Place: EcoTrack (smart composters tracking waste — sustainability + data tracking)
- 2023 1st Place: BagSHARE (reusable bag sharing — circular economy model)
- **Pattern:** focused, platform-based, sustainability solutions win at HTG

## (D) How Our Solution Works

### The 30-Second Pitch

> "EcoSync is a geo-local marketplace where contractors list surplus construction materials with a photo. Our AI identifies the material, suggests a price, and connects them with a nearby buyer — all while auto-tracking the carbon saved from every transaction."

### Three Core Features (Keep to Under 3 Min in Presentation)

1. **Smart Listing (10 seconds to post)**
   - Contractor takes a photo of surplus lumber/steel
   - AI identifies material type, condition, and suggests market price
   - Listing goes live with location, instantly visible to nearby buyers

2. **Geo-Matching (Hyper-local)**
   - Buyers within 20-mile radius see available surplus
   - Keeps transport emissions low and makes pickup practical
   - Map-based interface — see what's available near your job site

3. **Carbon Ledger (Automatic compliance)**
   - Every transaction auto-calculates carbon emissions saved (via EC3 data)
   - End-of-project export generates a compliance-ready report
   - Usable for Buy Clean permits, green building tax credits, LEED points

### Business Model (Simple, Proven)

| Revenue Stream | How | Price Point |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction fee | 5–10% commission per sale | ~$50–200 per transaction |
| SaaS subscription | Carbon reporting + permit vault | $49–199/month per project |
| Lead generation | Connect builders with sustainable material suppliers | Referral fee per lead |

### Why It's Credible

- **No hardware needed** — just a phone and a browser
- **Uses existing open data** (EC3 database) — not building carbon science from scratch
- **Geo-local model proven** by platforms like Craigslist, OfferUp, Facebook Marketplace — we're just vertical-specific
- **Regulatory tailwind** means demand will grow, not shrink

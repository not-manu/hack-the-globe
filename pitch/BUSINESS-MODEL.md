# EcoSync — Business Model (Single Slide)

## Revenue

**Phase 1 — Marketplace (Now)**
- **10% transaction fee** on every sale — revenue from day one
- **Carbon Reporting SaaS** ($49–199/mo) — compliance exports for Toronto's 70% diversion bylaw & Buy Clean permits
- **Featured listings** ($5–15) — sellers boost visibility for faster sales

**Phase 2 — Depots (Data-Driven Expansion)**
- Buy surplus at ~20–30% of retail, resell at 50–70% — **30–40% gross margins**
- Depots open only where request board data shows proven, concentrated demand

## Partnerships
- **EC3 Database** — 100K+ product EPDs powering our carbon tracking
- **Contractor associations (OGCA, OHBA)** — distribution to thousands of contractors
- **Municipal governments** — aligned with city diversion mandates; potential recommended channel

## Scalability
- Phase 1 is a web app — **near-zero infrastructure cost**, scales to any city
- GTA first (1.5M homes by 2031) → Hamilton, Ottawa → any city with a housing boom
- More construction = more surplus = more supply — **the crisis IS our supply chain**

## Risk Mitigation
- **Low supply?** Request board captures demand before supply exists — sellers see waiting buyers
- **Quality?** AI photo verification + ratings (Phase 1), human inspection at depot (Phase 2)
- **Competition?** Purpose-built — Kijiji/FB have no material search, no carbon tracking, no compliance reports
- **Bad depot placement?** We never guess — only expand where data proves demand
- **Materials expire?** They don't. Stone, brick, concrete, steel = zero spoilage, zero inventory loss

# Pitch Feedback

## Overall
The app was strong — mentors liked the product. The main feedback was to **double down on the pitch and the story**. The scope is too broad right now (landscaping, bricks, tubes, pipes, etc.). Pick 1–2 materials and go deep.

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Narrow the Focus
- Don’t try to cover every material. Trim down to 1–2 and research those markets thoroughly.
- Choose a market and double down on it. Emphasize product characteristics and quality.

### 2. Prove the Problem
- What is the market size? How big is the problem?
- How much material is lost or wasted today?
- Prove feasibility with data.

### 3. Pitch Flow
Structure the pitch as:
1. **What is it** — define the problem clearly
2. **Why we care** — market size, waste, and opportunity
3. **How we solve it** — the product (keep this under 3 minutes)

### 4. Market Angle
- Any reusable material can be resold, but consider **plastic** as a strong vertical — there’s already a huge resale market, many types of plastic, and significant price upside.
- Are there materials in usable condition that are currently being thrown away instead of resold?

## Round 2 Feedback — Warehouse Model

### Pivot: From Pure Marketplace to Collection & Resale Hub
Mentors suggested evolving the model beyond peer-to-peer:
- **Sellers** offload their unused surplus to us for cheap (better than paying tipping fees to dump it)
- **We collect/aggregate** materials at a warehouse or depot — acting as the middleman
- **We resell** in smaller, flexible quantities at a modest markup to people who need less (homeowners, small contractors, DIYers)

### Why This Is Stronger
- Solves the "chicken and egg" marketplace problem — we control supply
- Buyers get smaller quantities (you don’t need a full pallet, just 20 pavers)
- Sellers get a guaranteed buyer (us) instead of waiting for a match
- Warehouse model = inventory control, quality verification, trust

## Round 3 Feedback — Hybrid Model (Current Direction)

### Decision: Start Peer-to-Peer, Scale Into Depots
Rather than committing to a warehouse from day one (capital-heavy, risky), we go hybrid:

**Phase 1 — Community Marketplace + Request Board**
- Sellers list surplus directly, buyers post requests for what they need
- App geo-matches them. We take a 10% transaction fee.
- Zero infrastructure cost. This is what we demo at the hackathon.
- The request board captures demand even when supply is thin — solves cold start.

**Phase 2 — Depots Where Data Supports It**
- Every listing and request has a location. When we see concentrated demand in an area, we open a depot.
- Depots buy surplus from contractors, hold inventory, and resell in any quantity.
- Data-driven expansion — not guesswork.

### Why Hybrid Wins
- Starts lean (judges love low-risk, fast validation)
- The marketplace generates demand data that tells us where to place depots
- Natural evolution, not a pivot — Phase 1 becomes the demand signal for Phase 2
- Best of both: marketplace scales everywhere, depots solve quantity mismatch and quality trust where it matters most

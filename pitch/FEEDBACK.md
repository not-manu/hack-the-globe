# Pitch Feedback

## Overall
The app was strong — mentors liked the product. The main feedback was to **double down on the pitch and the story**. The scope is too broad right now (landscaping, bricks, tubes, pipes, etc.). Pick 1–2 materials and go deep.

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Narrow the Focus
- Don’t try to cover every material. Trim down to 1–2 and research those markets thoroughly.
- Choose a market and double down on it. Emphasize product characteristics and quality.

### 2. Prove the Problem
- What is the market size? How big is the problem?
- How much material is lost or wasted today?
- Prove feasibility with data.

### 3. Pitch Flow
Structure the pitch as:
1. **What is it** — define the problem clearly
2. **Why we care** — market size, waste, and opportunity
3. **How we solve it** — the product (keep this under 3 minutes)

### 4. Market Angle
- Any reusable material can be resold, but consider **plastic** as a strong vertical — there’s already a huge resale market, many types of plastic, and significant price upside.
- Are there materials in usable condition that are currently being thrown away instead of resold?

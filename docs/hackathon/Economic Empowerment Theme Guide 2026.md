Each hackathon project will be built around a specific theme - a focus area that outlines the kind of problems you’ll be solving and the impact your solution aims to create. Themes are designed to help guide your creativity, give structure to your ideas, and ensure your project is aligned with the overall goals of the event.

Your final project will be evaluated by a panel of judges who are experts in that theme’s domain. A strong alignment between your project and your chosen theme can significantly boost your score, as it shows you’ve understood the problem space, used relevant insights, and tailored your solution to the needs and opportunities in that area.

In short:

- Themes help you focus your ideas.
- Judges are specialists in the theme you choose.
- The closer your solution matches the theme’s goals, the better your chances of impressing the panel.

# Overview

‹#›

Rapid growth has lifted millions out of poverty, but progress has been uneven. Women are often held back more than men due to lack of access to paid work and education. Progress has been limited in regions like South Asia and sub-Saharan Africa, which account for 80 percent of those living in extreme poverty. New threats brought on by climate change, conflict and food insecurity, cement this need.  The use of finite resources is a topic covered by the economic aspects of development. 

How can we provide economic liberation for everyone? And Can we urgently collaborate and find solutions  to such an important need?

# Quality Basic Education

“Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.” – Nelson Mandela, First President of South Africa

Quality Basic education: Quality basic education refers to formal, non-formal, and informal public and private activities intended to meet the basic learning needs of people of all ages. It includes instruction at the first or foundation level, on which subsequent learning can be based, encompassing early childhood and primary (or elementary) education for children, as well as education in literacy, general knowledge, and life skills for youth and adults. (Inter-agency Network for Education in Emergencies )

Did you know?

“Around 90 percent of the world's population had completed a primary education in 2020, whereas only 66 percent had attained a secondary education.” (Statista)

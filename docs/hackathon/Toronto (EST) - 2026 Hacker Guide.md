# **➡️ 02 Overview of Hack The Globe & Schedule**

💡 Hack The Globe is a non-traditional **social impact hackathon** that promotes **multidisciplinary approaches** to solving pressing global issues

## **→ Social Impact Driven**

Students are invited to develop a **technologically-driven social enterprise** over **two weeks** to tackle the most pressing global development challenges.

## **→ A Multidisciplinary Approach**

Understanding the **cultural**, **social**, and **political** nuances of global challenges requires more than just a team of engineers. Students of **all backgrounds** are encouraged to participate.

A kickoff ceremony with a keynote speech to kick off an exciting two weeks. Hackers will also attend workshops, engage with sponsors and theme experts, meet their BCG mentors, and begin to ideate.

The long-awaited hackathon weekend\! **Hackers can start developing their solutions and presentation decks** on **Friday, March 20th at 11:59PM EST.** Programming also includes more workshops and office hours. Solutions/decks are due **Sunday, March 22nd at 8:00 AM EST,** and judging will begin at **9:30 AM EST**

**Problem Pitch 💡**

💡 **A problem pitch is designed to help scope problems and develop targeted solutions.** It is important to note that the problem identified in the pitch **does not** have to be the problem your team eventually chooses. It will consist of a **5-10 min** presentation of no more than **3-5 slides**.

**Q1: How do I look for problems?**

A: We encourage you to look for important problems in areas that have personally impacted you and also tie into Hack the Globe themes for 2026\. An important problem is one that is mission-critical to those affected and is often

* Repeated and urgently discussed  
* Systemic, affecting many different outcomes  
* Long-standing  
* Mission-critical

### **Q2: What’s the goal of the problem pitch?**

A: To help you and your team **develop a clear problem statement, ideate on possible solutions, and critically analyze their feasibility as well as identify potential blockers**. This is also a great opportunity for your team to practice your pitching/presentation skills and to get formal feedback from your BCG mentor.

### 

### 

### **Q3: When is the problem pitch due?**

A: Coordinate with your team and BCG Mentor to find a time that works for everyone before EOD March 20th.

However, **we recommend that you do your problem pitch by the end of the first week of the Ideation Period**. This way, you’ll have ample time to research and ideate based on feedback from your mentor and go into the Hackathon Weekend knowing exactly what your team wants to create.

### **A problem pitch presentation should contain some, if not all, of the following:**

| Problem Statement | Try to concisely describe the problem in 2-to-3 sentences. |
| :---- | :---- |
| Scale of the Problem | Which theme does the problem fall under? What industry or organizations are affected by the problem? |
| Context of the Problem | What are the drivers of the problem? Are there other external factors that contribute to the problem? |
| History of the Problem | Is there any attempt to solve the stated problem in the past? If so, why are/aren’t they successful? |
| Solutions | What are some possible solutions? |
| Feasibility Analysis | Are the above solutions feasible? What are some of the risks and mitigations associated with them? |

For any concerns regarding the problem pitch, please ask on the Slack Q\&A channel

# **☑️ 11 Evaluation Criteria: How to Succeed**

💡 **Solutions will be evaluated as: 45% business strategy \+ 45% technical implementation \+ 10% presentation \+ Bonus**

## **Submission Requirements**

1. **Pitch Deck** 🟨 ⬜  
   ***Submit a PDF or Powerpoint file that you will be using to present***  
   At a minimum, the deck should describe your (scope) problem, solution, and business/project plan. Put additional information (references, reports, data sources, revenue/cost models, technical details, etc.) in an appendices section.

2. **Technical Prototype** 📱💻  
   ***Submit a link to your Github Repo, Figma files, and/or additional technical components***  
   This is to verify that you began your technical implementation during the Hackathon. Winning teams may be subject to a code review by the organizers : )

**Teams will be evaluated based on these criteria**

| Business Strategy (45/100 points) | Technical Implementation (45/100 points) |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Problem and Solution Scope (15 points)** | **Minimum Viable Product (Total of 20 points)** |
| Well-developed, realistic plan that defines both the problem and the solution approach within a clearly scoped area. Integrates socioeconomic, environmental, political, and cultural considerations of the target population into the implementation strategy. | **Technical Proficiency (10 points)** Solution demonstrated a high level of technical skills (e.g. coding, CAD modeling, algorithms).  **Technology-Problem Fit (0 OR 10 points)** The solution is well-suited to the problem, demonstrating a strong fit between the approach and the challenge being addressed. |
| **Business Operation Model (15 points)** | **Technical Robustness (Total of 20 points)** |
| The business model can scale well and serve as a model for other efforts. Considers feasibility and partners and costs associated with scaling. | **Scalability (10 points)** The technical solution is designed to scale seamlessly and efficiently. It can handle increased demand, data, or users without degradation in performance.  **Innovation (10 points)**  Solution differentiates itself from others by using technology in a unique way to address the issue. **Tech Demo (5 points)** Teams use Devpost to demonstrate additional technical depth in a 2-3 minute segment.  |
| **Impact and Relevance (15 points)** |  |
| Addresses the target population’s needs, is supported by research on the problem’s scope and significance. Explains who benefits and how the solution creates meaningful impact. |  |
| **Presentation (10/100 points)** |  |
| **Presentation, Delivery, and Q\&A (10 points)** |  |
| Presentation slides are organized logically, concisely and clearly. The presentation tells a compelling story relevant to the problem pitch and the solution proposed. Presenters have clear and engaging delivery, and respond confidently and thoughtfully to judges’ questions. |  |

# **❔14 FAQ**

### **1\. Do I need to know how to code?**

Although coding skills are not required, it’s ideal to have a variety of experiences. Your technical demo can be a prototype (eg. Figma).

### **2\. Can I use any existing code?**

Preparing project-specific code ahead of time is not allowed. It’s acceptable to use libraries, frameworks or templates as long as they’re not the major part of your solution.

### **3\. Can I change my theme selection?**

Yes, you can change your theme anytime until the hackathon weekend. However, we encourage you to narrow in on a problem space during the two-week period to thoroughly research and scope your problem. Theme decisions will be officially due on March 20th.

### **4\. When am I able to come to the BCG Office?**

The office will only be available to Toronto in-person hackers during the Hackathon Weekend.
